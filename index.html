So, I decided to make a game where the player is a dog, so step one was to look at what “being a dog” means in game terms.

So I made a list of games with playable dogs that I had access to.

The main Games:

  • Okami
  • Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess
  • I’m just going to call it Twilight Princess
  • Monster Hunter Rise (Palamute)
  • Or just “Rise”

Smaller Games I took a look at since they had dogs in them and were 2D:

  • Miles & Kilo
  • Montaro / Montaro RE

Here is what I noticed from playing these games.

Movement:

Dogs are fast, and that’s shown off in the games I played. Whether it was Twilight Princess’s multiple speeds for wolf link, or the Palamute in Rise being primarily a speedy way around the map, or even Kilo being an auto-rup powerup in Miles & Kilo, dogs in the language of games are fast. So any game I make with a dog as a playable character is going to have to play with speed in some way.

However, I did notice a specific way speed was handled between games. What I’m going to call “speed levels.” While most of these dogs have a basic movement speed and a run speed, they also have a third level of speed, usually some kind of dash executed by running for a short time or doing some extra input. Thus the speed levels of “walk, run, dash”. And fittingly, these have their own animations taken from how dogs actually move.

With that out of the way, on to vertical movement. All of these dogs can jump, and from observing my own dog, this has to be a key aspect of movement. However, some games did things with the jump I feel the need to point out. Rise’s jump always gives forward momentum. This may be due to the Palamutre being a primarily movement tool, and thus the jump button being put on a trigger and generally not used from a standstill. This could be used to add to the feeling of speed that seems to be a key aspect of video game dogs.

In short:

My game’s dog will need to be able to walk, run, and dash. As well as jump, potential;ly giving some additional forward momentum.

Combat:

This is a bit of an interesting subject based on the games I picked. Okami’s combat is really focused around the ink brush and various arts from that. Rise’s Palamutes do have combat abilities while being ridden, but that’s not their primary reason, it’s kind of an extra. Montaro has no combat. And Miles & Kilo have a sonic style spin dash. So I’m basically left to look at Twilight Princess for combat queues. The issue there is wolf link’s combat isn’t the best. He has a 3 hit combo that moves him forward with lunging bites, a lock on attack, and a midna attack that locks on and then has link jump between locked on targets.

What I’m choosing to take from this is a 3 hit combo that moves you forward. That should feel dog-like enough, with the lunge style movement. It should also add to the general movement feel of the game I’m planning, having taken enough of the other aspects of movement into account.

Needed Abilities:

Digging:

Both Twilight Princess and Okami have digging as a core aspect of being a dog/wolf, and this makes sense. Dogs do like to dig. In Twilight Princess it’s used to find extra rupees as well as a movement tool, digging to get through bars and under walls. Whereas in Okami it’s used to hide things in the ground. Potentially for NPC quests.

So, Digging will have to be in the game, but could be a secondary mechanic. It might be worth putting in the extra time to think of something cool to do with digging, say a digging boss fight.

Barking:

Okami has a dedicated bark button, Twilight Princess has link bark whenever you enter dash. This one’s easy, even if it doesn’t do anything, the player will need to be able to bark on command. This could have gameplay impact, but even if it doesn’t this feature needs to be in the game.

Scent:

Dogs can smell better than humans, and while not all of the games I played showed that off, it was a key part of Twilight Princess. Wolf Link’s scent vision was used in some key parts of the game to track characters, or to find points to dig for some extra loot.

Small Design Brain Moment:

  • The way they handled the extra loot through scent is brilliant. It reinforces that the player has this ability by incentivising the player to use it to get some extra money. It’s also a way to reward the player for exploring, as some sections of the wolf link areas have digging spots only visible through scent hidden at the ends of optional corridors. So if the player wants to explore, they are rewarded and have a key mechanic reinforced.
  • There are some issues with it though, scent view restricts the field of view and can hide some aspects of the area, it can turn some wolf link sections into fairly linear “Follow the path” sections

Seeing as how much the dog that I’m basing this game around picks up scents and wants to hunt them, this is a needed mechanic in some form.

Extras:

  • NPCs in Okami can pet you. So yes, I will have to have some form of petting the dog.
  • Bones are healing items in Okami. Using dog items as items could be useful.
  • Twilight Princess has wolf link specific jumping puzzles involving Midna
  • Twilight Princess also had wolf block pushing puzzles, but that may just be a zelda thing
  • Dog needs to be able to sit. Potential Idle pose?
